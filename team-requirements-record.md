# ARABAI Team Requirements Record

Last updated: 2026-05-10

This document records the owner's concrete requirements and decisions for ARABAI, so future team members can continue the project with the same direction.

## 1. Brand And Positioning

- The site name is **ARABAI**.
- The purchased domain is **arabai.top**.
- The old working name **MyAI / MY AI / MYAI** must no longer appear on the public site.
- ARABAI means an AI guide made especially for Arabic-speaking users.
- The core positioning is:
  - Arabic-speaking users first.
  - Basic AI usage first.
  - Practical guide/manual, not a large corporate website.
  - Help ordinary people know AI, use AI, and let AI help with real work.
- The website should feel simple and clear, but carefully designed.
- Visual style should avoid pure white; use slightly warm/off-white gray tones.
- Font direction should reference the feeling of `aramco.com`, with Arabic-friendly typography.
- The site should not feel grand, corporate, or overbuilt. It should feel like a useful guidebook.

## 2. Working Languages And Live Languages

- The owner communicates mainly in Chinese and English.
- The final website must be bilingual:
  - Arabic as the primary audience language.
  - English as support/scaffold language.
- The initial content can be drafted in English first, then translated/adapted into Arabic.
- Arabic content should not feel like machine translation. It should be clear, plain, and suitable for ordinary Arabic-speaking users.

## 3. Homepage Requirements

- Homepage should stay simple.
- It should show:
  - ARABAI brand.
  - A clear one-line/small paragraph explanation.
  - Three first-level sections.
  - Why this website exists.
- The homepage should not flatten all content into one long page.
- Detailed content should appear only after the user chooses a first-level directory.
- Current main message direction:
  - Users hear about AI everywhere.
  - ARABAI explains AI in simple language for Arabic users.
  - AI should be easy to try, not hard to understand.

## 4. First-Level Structure

The first-level sections are:

1. **AI Beginner**
   - For users who do not know AI yet.
   - Explain AI, AI words, prompt, free vs paid, safety, common tools, and how to start.

2. **AI Advanced**
   - For users who already know AI exists and want to use it directly.
   - Organized by task, not theory.
   - Examples:
     - I want to write.
     - I want to make a plan.
     - I want to make slides.
     - I want to create images.
     - I want to make videos.
     - I want to make music.
     - I want to translate.
     - I want to summarize documents.
     - I want to grow my business.

3. **AI Expert**
   - For users who want one step deeper.
   - Must still use simple language.
   - Focus areas:
     - API.
     - API pricing.
     - AI Gateway.
     - Gateway platforms.
     - Gateway risks.
     - Credits.
     - Automation.
     - Team/business use.
   - This level is important because the future ARABAI business model may rely on API access and credit-based usage.

## 5. Content Style Requirements

- Content must be easy enough for a person who only knows how to type and has never used AI.
- Avoid programmer language unless the article is explicitly explaining a technical word.
- Even technical words must be explained by analogy.
- Beginner articles should explain each concept in one simple sentence when possible.
- Use story-like, analogy-driven explanation.
- The owner specifically liked the style of explaining AI like a capable assistant, waiter, kitchen, train station, or service window.
- Articles should form a closed learning loop:
  - A total beginner can understand AI.
  - The user can choose a tool.
  - The user can follow a real task.
  - The user can produce an actual result.
  - The user can understand free vs paid, safety, and why AI costs money.

## 6. Important Analogies

- **AI**
  - Like a capable assistant sitting beside the user.
  - It can write, plan, translate, draw, summarize, and give ideas.
  - But it needs a clear request because it cannot read the user's mind.

- **Prompt**
  - Like placing an order with a waiter.
  - If the order is vague, the result is vague.
  - If the order includes background, task, audience, style, and output format, the result is much better.

- **Large model**
  - Like a chef who has tasted millions of dishes.

- **Token**
  - Like a small bite of language.

- **Computing power**
  - Like the size of the kitchen and the strength of the stove.

- **API**
  - Like a service window behind a restaurant.
  - The website sends the user's order to AI in the background and brings the result back.

- **AI Gateway**
  - Like a train station.
  - One account or balance can reach many different AI trains/models.

- **Credits**
  - A simple wallet/receipt name for all the small AI costs happening behind the screen.

## 7. Advanced Articles Must Be Real Tutorials

- Advanced articles must not be empty talk.
- They must use real cases, real steps, screenshots when possible, and final results.
- A normal user should be able to follow the article and actually complete the work.
- Screenshots are only useful if they show a meaningful workflow and final output.
- A screenshot of ChatGPT merely describing a video/image/PPT is not enough.
- Required tutorial style:
  - Real case.
  - Tool used.
  - Prompt copied by user.
  - Step-by-step actions.
  - Screenshot/process evidence.
  - Final output.
  - What to check before using the output.

## 9. Video Creation Requirement

- General beginner video-generation method should be taught as:
  - Text to image.
  - Create a 9-grid storyboard.
  - Stitch the 9 images into a video.
- This method is preferred because it is easier for beginners to control than direct text-to-video.
- Video tutorials can include carefully selected external beginner videos/tutorials if real generation is not possible.

## 10. Links And Cross-Linking

- Articles should include clickable keywords.
- Example:
  - In AI Beginner, if ChatGPT is mentioned, the keyword can link to the AI Advanced article about ChatGPT.
  - In AI Advanced, if ChatGPT/API is mentioned, it can link to the AI Expert explanation.
- External links are allowed and useful.
- External videos/tutorials should be beginner-friendly and easy to understand.
- Official links should be used where safety matters, especially login, pricing, and tool pages.

## 11. Tool Rankings And Recommendations

- OpenRouter rankings were discussed, but OpenRouter does not cover every functional AI category needed by ARABAI.
- The project should use publicly available authoritative ranking or popularity sources when choosing representative tools.
- For each function, only one or two common tools are needed.
- Tools should not be separated into US vs China at the top level.
- Country/source can be mentioned in lower-level descriptions.

## 12. Commercial Model

The owner's long-term business direction:

- Free content attracts ordinary users.
- All published tutorials and guide content remain free.
- Do not create a paid tutorial membership for this site.
- Monetization can later come from:
  - Affiliate/referral/partner links to official tools.
  - ARABAI Credits wallet.
  - API-based access to multiple AI tools/models.
  - A future separate task platform where users post AI tasks and other people complete them for payment.
  - Possible future advertising.

## 13. ARABAI Credits Requirement

- Future service idea:
  - Users recharge a very small amount of money.
  - They can experience many latest AI technologies from one place.
  - They do not need to register many accounts or buy many subscriptions.
- Credits should be similar in spirit to:
  - OpenRouter credits/pay-as-you-go.
  - SiliconFlow multi-model API access.
  - Poe subscription/compute points concept.
- Current status:
  - Credits/recharge/payment must be marked **Coming Soon**.
  - Do not make real payment available yet.
  - Do not imply users can already buy credits.
  - Open Credits only after enough users are accumulated and payment, privacy, refund, account, API, and support rules are ready.

## 14. API Focus

- Expert level should explain why API matters.
- API is important because future ARABAI monetization may come from API access.
- However, this must not make the overall site feel technical.
- Explain API as a simple service window:
  - User clicks a task button.
  - ARABAI sends the request to AI through API.
  - AI returns result.
  - User pays through simple credits, not by understanding tokens or API keys.
- Official API platforms and gateway platforms should be explained clearly.
- Gateway risks must also be explained:
  - Privacy.
  - Uptime.
  - Billing surprises.
  - Provider lock-in.
  - Model availability.
  - Refund/support.

## 15. Conversion Philosophy

- The site should not feel too salesy.
- Owner does not want direct hard-selling language like:
  - "Pay now."
  - "Recharge here."
  - "If you do this often, pay."
- Only a small portion of users should be nudged toward future credits.
- Previous concept:
  - Around 5% of visitors may be guided toward recharge intent.
  - The site includes logic to avoid showing credit nudges too broadly.
- Credits should appear only when relevant and never as a forced pop-up/payment wall.

## 16. Community And Retention Requirements

- Owner wants to retain users after launch.
- Public message board was discussed, but a normal public board may become empty, noisy, or full of spam.
- Better approach:
  - Updates page.
  - Waitlist.
  - Feedback/suggestion form.
  - Later moderated community participation.
- Current site includes:
  - `community.html`
  - `ar-community.html`
- Future real collection methods can include:
  - Google Form.
  - Tally.
  - Mailchimp/Brevo.
  - WhatsApp Channel.
  - Telegram Channel.
  - Email list.
- Recommended initial fields:
  - Email or WhatsApp.
  - Language.
  - Main AI interest.
  - One question or contribution.

## 17. Community Points And Free Credits

New owner requirement:

- Users should eventually be able to speak/participate on the site.
- User activity can become a selling point.
- Active users may receive free Credits and use AI features for free.
- This should not be implemented as an uncontrolled public message board at first.
- Recommended mechanism:
  - Users submit questions, examples, suggestions, or corrections.
  - Contributions are reviewed.
  - Helpful participation earns Community Points.
  - Points may later convert to free Credits.
  - Abuse/spam should receive nothing.
- Example contribution types:
  - Ask a useful beginner AI question.
  - Share a real AI result.
  - Suggest a tool to explain.
  - Report a broken link or outdated tool.
  - Give feedback on unclear tutorials.
  - Write a short beginner tip.
- Current status:
  - The concept is published as **Coming Soon**.
  - No real points are issued.
  - No free Credits can be redeemed yet.
  - Terms mention future review, anti-spam, abuse limits, and redemption rules.
